When History met Culture...

Pune’s iconic Shri Kasba Ganapati, one of the most ancient temples in Maharashtra was installed by Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and Mata Jijabai in 1639. This temple and its surrounding Kasba Peth area is held in high esteem with lot of devotion from people all over Maharashtra as it is right in the heart of the city. Shri Kasba Ganapati - its local deity is known by many as the first known Ganapati idol that started off the prestigious 10-day Ganapati festival. This event is celebrated with lot of devotion and with a blend of cultural values and emphasis on social responsibilities that highlight the history of Maharashtra.

Sangharsh Dhol Tasha Pathak is known for its 3 main principles – Trust, Discipline and Sister-Brother bonding which we hold in very high esteem. We value our roots and our rich culture and these are the principles which have got us associated with one of the most famous Ganapati Mandals – Shri Kasba Ganapati in Pune.

For their prestigious procession that precedes the inauguration of the 10-day festival, Shri Kasba Ganapati in 2019 selected Sangharsh Dhol Tasha Pathak to join the procession along with another Dhol Tasha Pathak. We were 1 of the only 2 shortlisted of around 82 applicants who wanted to participate in this procession. It was a great honour and a start of something special for us.

On September 2, 2019, a red letter day for Sangharsh Dhol Tasha Pathak, we witnessed in heart of Pune City the merger of our rich culture and the history of the Manacha Pahila Shri Kasba Ganapati during a 3-hour long procession that crossed the heart of the city.

Our association with Shri Kasba Ganapati just got stronger after this day. We were proudly told that day by the management of the Shri Kasba Ganapati Mandal that they would want us back the following year as well. It was not just that. On January 28, 2020 during the Ganesh Jayanti festival which is celebrated with equal passion and festivity in Pune, Shri Kasba Ganapati invited us once again. So for the 2nd time inside 5 months, Sangharsh Dhol Tasha Pathak got the honour to salute and seek blessings from Pune's historic Shri Kasba Ganapati.

With the onset of COVID-19 and the fear of the pandemic, most celebrations during 2020’s Ganapati festival either got cancelled or were toned down. Shri Kasba Ganapati also honoured its commitment towards maintaining social responsibilities. On their special request, we visited the prestigious and historic Shri Kasba Ganapati Temple – the Gramdaivat which was the origin of Shri Kasba Ganapati’s history. At this historic venue on 22nd August 2020, with our small little team in its toned down volume but with renewed intensity and devotion, with all due consideration to safety norms over the COVID-19 fear, we paid our respects to our Lord.

Sangharsh Dhol Tasha Pathak feels blessed and we feel committed to come back strong in 2021...

हैद्राबाद

Sangharsh Dhol Tasha Pathak was already making waves by 2019, having just had a very successful and satisfying Ganeshutsav in Pune. That’s when Hyderabad came calling again and we had already been there twice before in 2015 and 2016. Our association with the people of Southern part of India was just about to get strong once more with Bijapur (3 times) in Karnataka from 2014, 2015, 2017 fresh in our minds, so off we were back on the way to Hyderabad.

This was going to be a challenge. Right from assembling a team with the peak of school/college exams approaching, those on jobs had to re-adjust work schedules, take time off and give a commitment. It was a mammoth exercise managing the operations part, right from finances, transport, our practice and getting all simple things in place. But we were upto it having had the experience of done this previously. It was once again our team work on display and nothing was left for last minute surprises.

Getting smarter with managing the transport and with a view to think forward, we went into multiple SUVs this time and not just 1 large bus for all. The idea was, we were to perform back-to-back on 2 days, not everyone could commit time for both days, some had to return early and then there were few or most of them who wanted to spend the extra day in Hyderabad to have a view of the beautiful city. Couple of our Tais and our 2 little ones could also stay just for a day to manage work and school commitments, we made sure, they got a decent and safe train journey arranged for the return.

Onwards to Hyderabad was as always a night journey which we always relished. Having homemade food which we all shared with relish at one of the stops we had for fuel, the journey felt just like a stroll despite the long distance. Early morning arrival into Hyderabad and the drive on the modern highways part of the NH network was a pleasant experience for many who were travelling here for the first time. We had a great breakfast on arrival arranged by our hosts, the traditional Idli and Sambar and it was a blessing, we could not forget. There were just a couple of hours we had for rest and changeover before getting ready for the procession of Goddess Durga which is worshipped with lot of tradition and lot of festivities in this part of India. We were fortunate to be a part of it.

Our Supari started around noon, the weather was fine although it was getting hotter by the day. We were greeted with lot of applause all along the way all through the day as we ended the procession round about 6 p.m. It also did rain and rained heavy for most of the afternoon and as was our tradition, we never stopped our performance. With our team well organised to provide the traditional shelter from rains using our famous ‘taadpatri’, we ensured the show never stopped.

Post evening, we were again treated to traditional Biryani by our hosts and it was a dinner that we all enjoyed. It was also time for us to say a goodbye to our Tais and the 2 little ones leaving us as they had a midnight train to board for Pune. The rest of us had a switchover to another part of Hyderabad where we were to perform the next day. The bodies were tired, but the enthusiasm never faltered nor did the spirits...

It was a pleasure being in Hyderabad on this special occasion and we all obliged with our rich traditional values on display... More was to follow the next day, but that’s another member’s story.....

Sangharsh.....
Ranjeet Bhatia
